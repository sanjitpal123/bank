Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about opening a business bank account in UAE.

Opening a business bank account in the UAE requires a valid trade license, passport copies of shareholders and directors, proof of address, and company documents. The bank then reviews your application, which typically takes 2–6 weeks. HS Advisors guides you through every step and helps ensure your application meets each bank's requirements.

Typically required documents include: UAE Trade License, Memorandum & Articles of Association, passport copies of all shareholders and signatories, Emirates ID (for UAE residents), proof of business address (tenancy contract or Ejari), and a business plan or company profile. Some banks may require additional KYC documents depending on your industry and business activity.

The approval process typically takes between 2 to 8 weeks, depending on the bank and the completeness of your documentation. With HS Advisors managing your application, we help minimize delays by ensuring everything is submitted correctly the first time, often reducing the process significantly.

Yes, foreigners and non-residents can open business bank accounts in UAE, provided the company is legally registered in the UAE. Many international entrepreneurs set up UAE free zone or mainland companies and successfully open corporate bank accounts. HS Advisors specializes in helping international clients navigate this process smoothly.

The best bank depends on your business type, nationality, and requirements. Popular choices include FAB (First Abu Dhabi Bank), ENBD (Emirates NBD), ADIB, RAK Bank, ADCB, and EIB. HS Advisors evaluates your specific profile and recommends the most suitable bank to maximize your approval chances.
