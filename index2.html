Common Questions About UAE Business Banking

Everything you need to know before opening your corporate bank account in UAE.

Opening a UAE business bank account requires a valid trade license, passport copies of all shareholders and directors, proof of business address, and KYC documents. Banks then conduct due diligence, typically taking 2–6 weeks. HS Advisors manages this entire process — from initial eligibility assessment through to account activation — leveraging direct bank relationships to ensure your application is presented in the strongest possible light.

Core documents include: UAE Trade License, Memorandum & Articles of Association, passport copies of all shareholders and authorized signatories, Emirates ID (for UAE residents), proof of business address (Ejari or tenancy contract), and a company business plan or profile. Some banks may request additional KYC based on your industry. Our team handles all preparation, certified translations, and notarizations.

The standard process takes 2–8 weeks depending on bank and documentation completeness. With HS Advisors managing your application — submitting through direct bank contacts and pre-validating all documents — clients typically receive approval within 2–4 weeks, often faster than applying independently.

Yes. Foreigners and non-residents can open UAE corporate bank accounts as long as the company is legally registered in the UAE (free zone or mainland). HS Advisors specializes in helping international entrepreneurs navigate this process and has an extensive track record with clients from 60+ nationalities.

The best bank depends on your business type, nationality, industry, and transaction needs. Popular options include FAB (First Abu Dhabi Bank), Emirates NBD, ADIB, RAK Bank, ADCB, and EIB. HS Advisors conducts a thorough assessment of your profile and recommends the specific bank and account product that maximizes your approval probability and fits your business needs.
